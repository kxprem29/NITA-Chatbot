The
Space Technology Incubation Centre
(S-TIC) at NIT Agartala
was
established on September 18, 2018, on the ground floor of the CSE building
(Room No. G-14), covering an area of 1600 sq. ft. The inauguration was held
remotely through online mode from the Hotel Leela Palace of Bengaluru by the
joint connotation of Prof. Harish Kumar Sharma, the then Director of NIT
Agartala and Dr K. Sivan, the then Secretary of the Department of Space and
Chairman of ISRO. This event had been glorified by the gracious presence of Sri
Biplab Kumar Deb the then Hon’ble Chief Minister of Govt. of Tripura.
This is
the first-ever Incubation Centre of the Indian Space Research Organization
(ISRO) throughout the India.
The ISRO
is desirous of setting up this Centre to attract and nurture the young academia
with innovative ideas / research aptitude for carrying out research and
developing the Academia-Industry ecosystem.
A total
of 10 Technology Development Projects (TDPs) were approved and sanctioned under
this Centre, among few are ready for a new start with active participation from
various Institutes of North-Eastern region.Various papers have been presented and
published/communicated in various International Journals and Conferences under
these Technology Development Projects.
I am
delighted to inform you that out of 10 (ten) approved & sanctioned incubation
projects under this Centre, 06 (six) are successfully completed.
As an
outcome of these completed projects, one Technology Transfer has been done by“Product Handover” to the Indian Space
Research Organization (ISRO), Department of Space, Government of India on April
27th, 2023.
This product has also been invited and showcased
in the “National Technology Week event” happened from 11th to 14th
May 2023 at Pragati Maidan of New Delhi. This event has been inaugurated and
circumambulated by the honorable Prime Minister of India Sri Narendra
Damodardas Modi.
