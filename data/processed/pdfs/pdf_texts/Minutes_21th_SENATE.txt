At the out set Prof. Gopal Mugeraya infiormed the house that NIT Agartala
regarding various activities and achievements of the Institute since the last SENATE
meeting successfully completed the admission process in both UG and PG programmes for
the academic session 2016-17. The admission in M.Tech programme was restricted to
GATE qualified candidates only who got seats through CCMT 2016. He further informed
the house that 39 candidates were admitted in Ph.D programme in January 2016 and the
maximum limit of guiding scholars by a particular supervisor might be reluxed in case of
supervisors of exceptional competence. It was also informed that around 30 Ph.D scholars
would be awarded Ph.D degree in the ensuing 9"" Convocation of the Institute. He also
- referred to the good campus placement of this Institute this year. Presently placement week
is being observed in the Institute. Apart from this Prof. Mugeraya mentioned that a good
number of subject experts visited this Institute during the last one year particularly at the
time of Oral Examination of Ph.D scholars and the Institute was enriched in a big way from
their academic ideas, interactions with students, scholars and faculties. Prof. Mugeraya
requested all the Heads of the Departments to identify 3 (three) best areas of their individual
department on which they could be working for the next 5(five) years. The performance of
this Institute was reviewed by an external team and their report was very good. This Institute
provided Vocational Training to 40 rural students of the adjoining area of NIT Agartala.
This Institute was paying scholarship to 10 (ten) ITI students and proposed to increase this
attempt further in the years to come. He also informed the house that recently fund flow
from the Govt. of India was slowed down and the Institute resorce generation was also
reduced. In spite of this, all the previously started projects were either completed or would
be completed soon. With this remark he requested the Registrar to place the agenda points
for discussion and also requested the members to join the discussion with their valuable
ideas.

 

Dr. D. Bhattacharya, Registrar (i/c) then welcomed all the members and placed the
agenda points in the meeting one after another. Dr. Ajoy Kumar Das, Dean (AA) also pleced
certain agenda points before the house.
