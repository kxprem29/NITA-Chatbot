ABOUT THE CONFERENCE:
Postmodernism has dominated the cultural sphere since the 1960s, and it has
become an embedded umbrella term that pervades the theoretical framework
of contemporary critical analysis. Alan Kirby in an influential essay, 8Death of
Postmodernism and Beyond9 (2006) has proclaimed the shift from
postmodernism to 8Digimodernism9, commensurate with the digital revolution
that characterises 21st century life and culture. The critical theories [viz. post-
postmodernism (Turner), meta-modernism (Vermeulen and van den Akker),
trans-postmodernism (Epstein)] that have proliferated in the last two decades,
to characterize an era in the 8aftermath9 of postmodernism; categorically or
tangentially (albeit, ironically!), fall under the rubric of the postmodern. Even
the critics who ostensibly distance themselves from the tenets of
postmodernism, take it as a point of departure. The gargantuan shadow of
postmodernism undeniably encroaches upon all forms of cultural phenomena
still. It is therefore, imperative to interrogate the predominance as well as the
waning effects and traces of the seemingly ubiquitous interpretive lens of
postmodernism. Is cultural criticism still entangled in the deeply rooted matrix
of postmodern critical methodology? How has the locus of identity
metamorphosed in tandem with the sociocultural transformations? This
international conference would attempt to deliberate the (dis)/continuities of
the postmodern turn to determine the locus of contemporary cultural mores
vis-Ã -vis the theoretical modes of criticism. We are thus encouraging you to
8unravel9 (in the true sense of the term) the behemoth of postmodernism, so as
to (or not to!) reach towards a critical consensus.
CONFERENCE SUB THEMES:
Posthumanism, Post-theory, Post-feminism, Postcolonialism, Poststructuralism,
Digital Humanities, Trauma Studies, Cultural Memory Studies, Media Studies,
Medical Humanities, Popular Culture, Critical Theory, Film Studies, Cultural

The call for papers is however, not confined to the central theme of the

ABOUT NIT AGARTALA:
NIT Agartala is a centrally funded Institution set up to impart
quality technical education to various levels of higher learning.
This institute is one of the 31 National Level Technical Institutes
set up by the Govt. of India. The Institute9s mission is "To help to
improve the economic development of the country particularly the
North-East states and also the public systems, through the pursuit
of excellence in technical education, research, consultancy, and
training". The Institute is spread over an area of 392 acres and is
well connected by surface rail, air, and national highway. NIT
Agartala is ranked among the 100 top institutions for the last three
consecutive years in NIRF.
ABOUT THE DEPARTMENT OF MANAGEMENT, HUMANITIES & SOCIAL SCIENCES:
The Department has upgraded into the Department of
Management, Humanities & Social Sciences with effect from the
academic session 2019-2020. The Department of Management,
Humanities & Social Sciences was established with the objective of
making an engineer enlightened with adequate knowledge of
his/her surroundings. The approach of the Department is
interdisciplinary. The Department offers undergraduate courses to
B.Tech students in Economics, Costing, Management Principles,
Management, Business Communication, and Communicative Skill
Development and full-time graduate program in Management.
Besides that, it encourages the students to actively take up
research in various fields of Humanities, Social Sciences and
Management. The Ph.D. program of the Department in Economics,
Literature, and Management has been introduced to serve that very
purpose. Along with these diverse academic departments, the
Department is home to a wide variety of interdisciplinary
collaborations, path-breaking research projects, and unique areas
of study. The ultimate objective of any education system is to
produce human capital who will be responsible citizens, besides
excelling in his/her own domain. To produce a complete
technocrat, only technical knowledge seldom serves the purpose.
He/She has to be socially aware with a compassionate human
faculty.

GUIDELINES FOR PAPER SUBMISSION:
Abstracts of 150-250 words with 5-6 keywords on any of the above themes or
any other related topics in original in English are invited for the conference.
The full length of the paper should not be less than 3000 words. The first page
of the abstract/paper should contain the title of the paper, Name of the
Author(s), Affiliation, Address with email id, and mobile number. All the
papers would be reviewed by the technical committee members of the
conference before its final acceptance and it will undergo a plagiarism check
in order to maintain the academic ethics. The abstract and the full paper for
the conference can be submitted to the conference email ID:
conference2023.mhssnita@gmail.com

reputed publisher. The acceptance of the papers to be published would be
intimated individually by email, after the conference. The details of the
publication (name of the publisher, publication fee, tentative schedule, etc.)
will be informed afterwards.
