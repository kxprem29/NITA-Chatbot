Technical Specifications for an integrated set up under Conversion analysis of the syngas production

from single-use absorbent waste materials and its applicability for power generation in
industries/hospitals through RCCI combustion comprises of

-> Data acquisition system with piezo sensors and piezo powering unit

-> Electronic Control Unit for engine test rig N
-> Emission Analyser ,

-> Single Cylinder four stroke Diesel Engine test setup with eddy current dynamometer

The equipment should consist of the following:

1. Engine: single-cylinder, four-stroke, water-cooled design, with a displacement range of
500-1000 cc and a self-start arrangement.

2. Modified to RCCI Engine [(Waste to Energy (WTE): Gas Produced from Waste/Bio-
waste; green /grey Hydrogen/ Acetylene gas/Methane engine)] up to 7.5 Ps to 10Ps or

ew better. Include an exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) assembly with EGR rate measurement

. and a control system for NOx control.

3. The engine's programmable open ECM system should support RCCI hydrogen
operation, dual fuel methane/acetylene/alcohol operation, and low-temperature
combustion (LTC) mode with dedicated RCCI engine operation

4. The test rig should have a dedicated urea/methanol injection system into the exhaust,

with adjustable quantity variation. i

5. RCCI engine should be capable of operating in dual fuel and LTC modes for constant
torque applications

6. Engine in the test rig should be capable to run on different fuels, including but not
limited to diesel, biodiesel, alcohol, hydrogen, Waste to Energy (WTE): gas produced
from waste/bio-waste, Liquefied Petroleum Gas. (LPG), Compressed Natural Gas
(CNG) and Acetylene gas. Overall, the test rig is to be configured to ran on RCCI modes
of operation with Open ECU systems, urea injection system. All the necessary injections
systems for injecting liquid and gaseous fuel along with all necessary safety features
(particularly for hydrogen gas) must be integrated in the test rig.

7. The engine should be coupled with an eddy current dynamometer capable of handling a
loading capacity 10-20% higher than the engine's power output.

8. The necessary gas injection system, including manifold/port and gas cylinders, should be
provided. The gas cylinder should have a capacity of 65-70 liters or more, filled with 10
kg or more of gas, with a working pressure of 335-350 bar or more and a delivery
pressure of 100-150 PSI or more. The system should also include a regulator, refill valve,
stand, trolley for easy transportation, delivery and tank pressure gauges, and dry and wet
flame arrestors.

9. Manifold/port/TBI liquid injection system: A second fuel tank with a liquid injection
system is required. It should include a low-pressure pump, wiring harness, ECU, and a
second fuel (liquid) injection system with high-impedance saturated injectors. The system
should be capable, through software control, of providing peak and hold current.
Additionally, a burette with a stand and fuel flow measurement capability should be
included.
