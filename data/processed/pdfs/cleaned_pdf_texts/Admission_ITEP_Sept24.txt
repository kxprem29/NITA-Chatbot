III. Eligibility:

a) The candidate should pass class XII Board Examination (10+2) or its equivalent examination securing a minimum of 50% marks in aggregate (best five subjects, including Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics) from a recognized Board/Council.

And

b) To be eligible, it is mandatory that the candidate should have studied and passed Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics subjects in class XII Board Examination (10+2) or its equivalent examination.

And

c) The candidate should be qualified in NCET-2024, conducted by NTA.

And

d) State of eligibility (HS or OS):

i. State of eligibility means the state from where the candidate has passed Class XII (or equivalent) examination. It is to note that the state of eligibility DOES NOT depend upon the native place or the place of residence of the candidate.

ii. If a candidate has passed Class XII (or equivalent) examination from one state but appeared for improvement from another state, the candidate’s state of eligibility will be from where the candidate first passed the Class XII (or equivalent) examination and NOT the state from where the candidate has appeared for improvement.
iii. For Open Schooling candidates, the state of the center will be considered as the State of Eligibility.

IV. Online application:

All applicants must apply online at https://admission.nita.ac.in/

The portal is compatible with Mozilla Firefox. Candidates are advised to use Mozilla Firefox browser only.

Candidates should apply through online mode only. A non-refundable application fee of Rs. 1000/- for OP/OBC-NCL/EWS candidates and Rs. 500/- for SC/ST/PwD candidates has to be submitted through the online payment portal only.

Offline, handwritten, or printed applications will not be entertained.

After registration, an Application ID (to be used as login name) and Password will be sent to the email ID provided by the applicant.

Using the login credentials, applicants must login to the Admission Portal for filling in the required information.

Application fees (non-refundable) must be paid through this online portal only.

After completion and submission of the online form, the filled form in PDF format will be generated.

On the day of reporting, candidates should bring a printed copy of the online filled-in form along with all the originals of the necessary documents (to support all educational qualifications, date of birth, category, NCET- 2024 score card etc.) and a set of photocopies of all such documents, duly self-attested.

NIT Agartala will not be responsible for any failure on the part of the candidates in making payment of the application fee online for any reason.

V. Documents to be uploaded (Scanned copy of Original):
(Multiple documents to be converted into a Single PDF file and uploaded wherever applicable)

1.  Valid score card of NCET-2024 (mandatory).
2.  10th standard/Matriculation or equivalent certificate or any other certificate issued by government authorities, as proof of Date of Birth and Name of Candidate (mandatory).
3.  Mark sheet/grade card and Pass certificate (if issued separately from school) of the class XII Board examination (10+2) or its equivalent examination (As single PDF file) (mandatory).
4.  For proof of State of eligibility:

Mark sheet/grade card and Pass certificate (if issued separately from school) of the class XII Board examination (10+2) or its equivalent examination (As single PDF file) in case the name and address of school is clearly written on it. In case of applicant passing from Open School, admit card of 12th examination (mandatory).

5.  Conduct/Character Certificate issued by the Institution last attended.
6.  SC/ST/PwD/OBC-NCL/EWS certificate (if and whichever applicable).
The OBC-NCL/ EWS certificate must be issued by a Competent Authority on or after 01/04/2024. Admission under the PwD category will be based on the recommendation of the Medical Board of NIT Agartala.

Important Note:

*   Caste certificate (SC/ST/OBC-NCL) issued by Maharashtra State must be validated by the Social Welfare department (in case of SC and OBC-NCL category) and Tribal Welfare department (in case of ST category) of Maharashtra Government.
*   SC/ST/OBC-NCL candidates of Maharashtra State have to produce their Caste Validity Certificate.
*   ST certificates from Tamil Nadu state must be issued by the concerned Revenue Divisional Officer.
*   If any Certificate is in a language other than English/Hindi, an English version of that certificate attested by a Gazetted Officer/Competent Authority should be uploaded compulsorily, along with a scan copy of the original certificate in a Single PDF file.

VI. Selection Procedure:

*   Based on the total score obtained in NCET-2024.
*   In case of any tie, the candidate older in age will be placed above the candidate who is younger in age. If the tie-breaking criterion fails to break the tie, then, higher marks in the total marks obtained in Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry papers in the Higher Secondary level will be considered as a tie-breaker.
