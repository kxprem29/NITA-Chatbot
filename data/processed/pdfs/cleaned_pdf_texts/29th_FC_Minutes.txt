Minutes of the 29th Meeting of the
Finance Committee
held on March 24, 2017

The 29th meeting of the Finance Committee of the National Institute of Technology, Agartala, was scheduled to be held on March 24, 2017, at 12 noon in the Conference Room of the International Guest House, NIT Agartala. However, at the request of the members, the meeting was preponed to 11 am, which was communicated to all members in advance.

The following members were present at the meeting:

(1) Prof. Deepak B. Phatak: Chairperson, Finance Committee and BoG of NIT Agartala.

(2) Prof. Gopal Mugeraya: Member, Director, NIT, Agartala.

(3) Shri K. Rajan: Under Secretary, MHRD, Govt. of India (Representative of Special Secretary/Additional Secretary/Joint Secretary dealing with Technical Education, Dept. of Higher Education, MHRD).

(4) Dr. John Deb Barma: Member, Asst. Professor, NIT, Agartala.

(5) Dr. Debasish Bhattacharya: Member Secretary, Finance Committee and Registrar (I/C), NIT, Agartala.

The Financial Advisor (FD), MHRD, could not attend the meeting due to prior commitments.

Prof. Deepak B. Phatak, Chairperson of the Finance Committee of NIT Agartala, presided over the meeting and welcomed all members of the Finance Committee, thanking them for attending.

The Chairperson then invited the Director and the Registrar to initiate a discussion on the agenda items.

At the outset, the Director apprised the members regarding the achievements of the Institute in the academic arena and other activities. He then requested the Registrar to present the agenda items.

Item No. 29.1: Confirmation of the Minutes of the 28th Meeting of the Finance Committee held on July 01, 2016.

The Finance Committee confirmed the minutes of its 28th meeting held on July 01, 2016, at NIT Transit House, New Delhi.

Item No. 29.2: Action taken on the decision of the 27th Meeting of the Finance Committee held on December 05, 2015, in the Conference Room of NIT Transit House, New Delhi.

The Finance Committee noted the action taken report with the following observation:

(i) With respect to Sl. No. 13 of the Action Taken Report on Item 27.13 of the 27th Finance Committee, the Finance Committee, as per the suggestion of the Chairperson, agreed to consider a special agenda on the Campus Wide Networking expansion issue, which would be the last agenda item.

Item No. 29.3: To consider the Financial Status of the Institute as on February 28, 2017.

The Registrar apprised the committee that after February 28, 2017, the Institute had received a further installment of Rs. 150.00 lakhs from MHRD under Non-Plan, thus totaling the Non-Plan Grant up to March 24, 2017, to Rs. 2600.00 lakhs. IRG/Interest earned as of 24-03-2017 is Rs. 2364.64 lakh, and the actual expenditure under Non-Plan as of 23-03-2017 is Rs. 5017.81 lakhs. Therefore, the Institute urgently needs Rs. 600.00 lakhs to meet the expenditure up to 31-03-2017, including the salary of the employees and provision for Pension and Retirement benefits for the month of March 2017.

The Committee requested the MHRD representative present at the meeting to look into the demand on an urgent basis.

Item No. 29.4: Present Status of TEQIP-II Project of NIT Agartala.

The Committee expressed its satisfaction with the progress of the TEQIP-II project at NIT Agartala. It was noted that 93% of the grants received from NPIU had been utilized by the Institute.

Item No. 29.5: To consider the Physical & Financial Statement of the ongoing construction works/projects.

The Committee members perused the Physical & Financial Statement of the ongoing construction works/projects and the priority attached by the Institute to various works. The Committee suggested that networking of the Institute campus should be made another priority.

Item No. 29.6: To consider placing the order for execution of the work of “Up-gradation of the internal main road with both side footpath including side drain, electric post, bus stop, sitting arrangement at a certain interval & conduit from main gate-I to auxiliary gate-2 via housing and boys hostel & auxiliary gate-2 to main gate-I and auditorium to existing play ground (old) at NIT Agartala” at a tender value of Rs. 12,16,37,422.00, the estimated cost of the work being Rs. 9,29,18,776.00.

Item No. 29.8: To consider the Audit Report on the accounts of the Institute for the year 2015-16.

The Finance Committee perused the Audit Report on the accounts of the Institute for the year 2015-16 and the para-wise reply of the SAR for the year 2015-16, forwarded to AG Tripura by the Institute.

Item No. 29.9: TDS liabilities of Rs. 1,52,92,993 for payment made to Tripura Housing and Construction Board, Agartala, during the financial year 2007-08 to 2010-11.

The matter was discussed at length. It emerged during the discussion that out of the TDS Liabilities of Rs. 1,52,92,993, the TDS amount in default is Rs. 97,85,947, and the Interest part is Rs. 55,07,046/-. The Committee recommended that the TDS amount in default, amounting to Rs. 97,85,947/-, may be immediately paid to the Income Tax Department, Agartala, and the Institute should file a fresh appeal before the appellate authority for waiving the accrued interest.

Further, the Tripura Housing and Construction Board should be informed that, in order to mitigate further Interest liability on TDS, NIT Agartala decided to deposit the TDS amount. However, the whole liability lies with the Tripura Housing and Construction Board. The Tripura Housing and Construction Board should also be informed to refund the TDS liability, together with the interest accrued, to NIT Agartala at the earliest. Also, regarding the counter appeal filed by ITO (TDS), Agartala, against a relief of Rs. 84,36,000/- allowed to NIT Agartala, the Finance Committee suggested that the Institute should defend itself properly. Tripura Housing & Construction Board may be pursued to refund the TDS liability, together with the interest accrued, to NIT Agartala. The decision being vital, the Finance Committee confirmed the decision for implementation.

Item No. 29.12: Any other item with the permission of the Chair.

Item No. 29.12.1: To consider approval for the work of “Supply, installation, testing & commissioning of 14 (fourteen) lifts in Administrative Building 1 & 2, School of Management, International Guest House, Academic Block (ECE Dept. & DoNER) and Central Library at NIT Agartala” at a cost of Rs. 4,17,13,490.00.

The issue of installation of 14 (fourteen) lifts in the newly constructed buildings of NIT Agartala was discussed at length. The Committee noted that, despite 3 (three) nationwide open tendering processes initiated by the Institute, no firm could be selected due to poor participation of bidders. The Building & Works Committee (BWC) in its 38th meeting (item no. 38.5) held on 15-03-2017, considering the situation, approved the proposal to award the work of installation of 14 (fourteen) nos. of lifts to CPWD as deposit work with an estimated cost of Rs. 4,17,13,490.00. Under the circumstances, and in view of the prolonged difficulties being expressed by PwD students, employees, and elderly persons in climbing beyond the 4th floor in some buildings, the Finance Committee recommended the proposal of the Building & Works Committee as stated above to the Board. Provisions of GFR, CVC guidelines & Govt. of India instructions may be followed. The decision taken in this regard is also confirmed.

Item No. 29.12.2: To adopt and approve the manual of NIT, Agartala Medical Attendant Rules in line with CS (MA) Rules.

After the last Finance Committee meeting, the above issue was again considered in the 42nd meeting of the BoG held on December 27, 2017, in the Conference Room, First Floor, Shastri Bhavan, New Delhi. The Board's decision is as follows:

“The Director, IFD, Dept. of Higher Education, MHRD, Govt. of India, informed the Board that the MHRD, Govt. of India, is going to issue a letter to all NITs to follow CS (MA) rules of Govt. of India for Medical Benefits to regular employees of NITs. Therefore, CS(MA) rules be followed and, if required, minor adaptations in line with CS(MA) rules be made depending upon the facilities in the North-East Region.”

The Finance Committee noted the decision of the Board.

Item No. 29.12.3: To consider and adopt a Group Health Insurance Policy for all categories of employees and their family members of NIT, Agartala.

The Finance Committee recommended the proposal of adopting a Group Health Insurance Policy for all categories of employees (Regular and Contractual) to the BoG for consideration. The Insurance provider should have a claim settlement procedure at Agartala. There will be no financial commitment on the part of NIT Agartala, since the premium contribution will be deducted from employee’s salary. However, there should not be any Financial liability on the part of NITs/MHRD.

Item No. 29.12.4: To consider the proposal of signing an MoU with the National Environmental Engineering Research Institute (NEERI), Nagpur.

For the sake of promoting academic and research co-operation, the Finance Committee recommended the proposal of signing an MoU with the National Environmental Engineering Research Institute (NEERI), Nagpur, to the BoG.

Item No. 29.12.5: Appraisal to the Finance Committee regarding the signing of MoUs with IIT Madras for research & academic collaboration.

The Finance Committee perused the proposal and recommended the same to the BoG for approval.

Item No. 29.12.8: To consider the formation of a Selection Committee for the Start-up programme of Govt. of India and selected start-up proposals.

The Dean (R&C) was invited during the meeting for briefing on the Status of the Start-up programme. After discussion, the Finance Committee recommended the proposal to the BoG for approval. The cost will be met from the DST grant and the Institute grant on an equal sharing basis. The total cost of the approved eight projects is Rs. 40 lakhs. A committee may be formed to note the status and the benefits to be achieved in respect of the start-ups.

Item No. 29.12.9: To consider the work of Electromechanical work in the swimming pool of the sports complex at NIT Agartala.

The Director apprised the committee that the Sports Complex of the Institute has a provision for a swimming pool. However, the Electromechanical work needed for the filtration and purification of water in the Pool was not included in the original BOQ of the project. The other civil works needed have been completed. Now the Sports Complex is complete in all respects apart from the Electro-Mechanical Works of the Swimming Pool. The Institute proposes to execute the work as deposit work by CPWD. The CPWD has also agreed to the same.

Then the Committee, after thorough discussion, recommended the proposal subject to the provision of GFR and Govt. of India instructions of awarding the work to CPWD as deposit work for consideration of the BoG, for the sake of time saving and early completion of the work.

Item No. 29.12.10: To consider the expansion of Campus Wide Networking (Phase III) of NIT Agartala.

As decided during the discussion on Item No. 29.2 on the Action Taken Report, the Finance Committee recommended the Institute proposal for the expansion of Campus Wide Networking (Phase III) of NIT Agartala for existing infrastructure and upcoming infrastructure under consideration by awarding the work to NICSI (National Information Centre Services Incorporated). However, it was opined that the mode of similar work carried out in NIT Patna be referred to for a detailed process and implementation procedure. It is also advised to look into similar practices from other NITs (such as Patna as well as other NITs). The committee also confirmed its decision in this regard.

The meeting ended with a vote of thanks to the Chair.
